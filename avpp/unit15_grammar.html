1. What will happen to the Kovilara Paricchattaka Tree when it is dry in leaf?
When it is dry in leaf, there will be leaf-fall.
2. What will happen to the tree after its leaf-fall?
After its leaf-fall, every bud will appear soon.
3. What will happen when the buds appear?
When the buds appear, the shoots will come out soon.
4. What will happen when the shoots come out?
When the shoots come out, the blossoms will form soon.
5. What will the flowers be like?
The flowers will be shaped like the red lotus.
6. What will the flowers be like when they are lotus-shaped?
When they are red lotus-shaped, the heart of the flower is closed but the outer petals are wide open.
7. How do the Thirty-Three Devas feel from the time that the divine tree is dry in leaf until its blossoming time?
They feel delighted during that time.
8. Why do they long for the blossoming time of the divine tree?
They long for it because it is the time for them to enjoy the five sense-pleasures. or: They long for it because it is the time when they enjoy the five sense-pleasures.
9. What do they do during the blossoming time?
They sport for four divine months at the foot of the tree and indulge in the five sense-pleasures during the blossoming time.
10. How far does the radiance of the tree pervade?
Its radiance pervades fifty yojanas.
11. How far is its perfume carried by the wind?
Its perfume is carried a hundred yojanas by the wind.
12. What is the time when the Ariyan disciple is dry in leaf?
The Ariyan disciple is dry in leaf when he is inclined to go forth from home into a homeless life.
13. What is his leaf-fall?
His leaf-fall is the time when he has his hair and beard shaved off and put on the yellow robe and gone forth.
14. When do his buds appear?
His buds appear when he is aloof from sense-pleasures, from evil states of mind, he enters on and abides in the first Jhana, a state of joy and ease born of detachment, accompanied by applied thought and sustained thought.
15. When do his shoots come out?
His shoots come out when he enters on and abides in the second Jhana, a state of joy and ease, born of concentration without applied and sustained thought, a state of inward calm and one-pointedness of mind.
16. When do his blossoms form?
His blossoms form when he is aloof from joy, dwelling detached, mindful and composed, (he) experiences in his body that ease of which the Arahants say: ‚ÄúThe one who has equanimity and is mindful dwells at ease‚Äù, and (he) enters on and abides in the Third Jhana.
17. When are his flowers red lotus-shaped?
His flowers are red lotus-shaped when he gets rid of ease and pain, ends his former pleasures and sorrows, enters on and abides in the fourth Jhana, a state of pure equanimity and mindfulness without pain and ease.
18. When are his flowers in full bloom?
His flowers are in full bloom when he destroys the cankers, enters on and abides in full realisation, Nibbana.
19. What do the earth devas do then?
They shout that this revered sir, living the life of faith, has destroyed the cankers and dwells in full realisation.
20. What do the Devas in the Heaven World do next?
They cry back that this revered sir, living the life of faith, has realised by his super-knowledge here and now the emancipation of mind and emancipation of wisdom without cankers, enters on and abides in it.
21. How far does their sound soar up?
Their sound soars up to the Brahma heaven.
22. What simile did the Buddha use to describe the progressive power of the Ariyan disciple?
He used the simile of the Divine power of the Kovilara Paricchattaka tree from the time it is dry in leaf until its blossoming time to describe the progressive power of the Ariyan disciple.
23. Who is the only person born into the world for the welfare of many..., out of compassion for the world?
It is a Tathagata, an Arahant, a Fully Self-Awakened One who is the only person born into the world for the welfare, the happiness of many folk, out of compassion for the world, for the profit, welfare and happiness of devas and mankind.
24. Whose appearance is hard to be found in the world?
It is the appearance of a Tathagata, an Arahant, a Fully Self-Awakened One that is hard to be found in the world.
25. Who is born into the world as a wonderful man?
It is a Tathagata, an Arahant, a Fully Self-Awakened One who is the only person born into the world as a wonderful man.
26. Whose passing away is to be regretted by many folk?
It is the passing away of a Tathagata, an Arahant, a Fully Self-Awakened One that is to be regretted by many folk.
27. Who is unique, without a peer,... best of all in the world?
It is a Tathagata, a Fully Self-Awakened One who is unique, without a peer, without a counterpart, incomparable, unequalled, matchless, unrivalled, best of all in the world.
28. Whose appearance is the appearance of a mighty eye, a mighty light, a mighty radiance, of six unsurpassed things?
It is the appearance of a Tathagata, an Arahant, a Fully Self-Awakened One that is the appearance of a mighty eye, a mighty light, a mighty radiance, of six unsurpassed things.
