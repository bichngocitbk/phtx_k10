Question Answer (click to reveal)
1. Did the prince become bored of his royal luxury?
Yes, he did. He became bored of it after a time.
2. What was he seeking for?
He was seeking for mental peace.
3. What did he desire to do one day?
He desired to make a journey around the city.
4. What did the King do in order to please his son?
He ordered his people to make necessary arrangements and decorations all over the city.
5. What did he see on his journey around the city on the first three days?
On his journey around the city, he saw an old man, a sick man and a dead man.
6. Why was he shocked by these sights?
He was shocked because it was the first time that he had seen them.
7. What did he realize then?
He realized that no one could escape those pains and sorrows.
8. What sight satisfied him finally?
The sight of a holy hermit with a bright and peaceful look satisfied him at last.
9. How did he feel for mankind?
He felt compassionate for mankind who had to face those sufferings.
10. What did he decide to do?
He decided to find a way out of all sufferings.
11. When did he leave his kingdom?
He left his kingdom and renounced the world on a full moon day at midnight.
12. What was his going forth called?
It was called the Great Renunciation in 594 BC.
13. Where did the ascetic Gotama wander?
He wandered about the valley of the Ganges.
14. Who did he approach at first?
At first he approached the famous teachers: Alara Kalama and Uddaka Ramaputta.
15. Why did he turn away from them?
He turned away from them because their methods didn't lead to Superknowledge, Awakening.
16. Who did he join next?
He joined a group of five ascetics.
17. What did he practise for 6 years?
He practised the most severe ways of life for six years.
18. Why did he lose all his strength?
He lost all his strength because he gave up food.
19. Did he reach his goal after too much suffering?
No, he didn't. He didn't reach his goal after so much suffering.
20. What path did he follow at last?
He followed a middle path.
21. Who offered him the milky rice?
A young lady called Sujata offered it to him.
22. Where did he sit in meditation?
He sat in meditation under the big Bodhi tree (assatha tree, a fig tree).
23. Why couldn't Mara and the evil spirits disturb him?
They couldn't disturb him because he was very resolute in his great struggle for Buddhahood.
24a. What kind of knowledge did he achieve at last?
He achieved the Three-fold Knowledge.
24b. What does it mean?
It is the knowledge composed of 3 parts: the knowledge of his own former births, the knowledge of other beings' passing away and arising, and the knowledge of the total destruction of all impurities.
25. What kind of freedom did he attain when he became the Buddha?
He attained the freedom of mind and the freedom of wisdom when he became Samma Sambuddha.
26. What does the Buddha mean?
It means the Fully Self-Awakened One.
27. What did the Buddha do for the good of mankind?
He showed the way to the end of all sufferings, Nibbana, true happiness.
28. What does the Door of Deathlessness symbolize?
It symbolises the Path leading to the end of sufferings, Nibbana, supreme happiness.
29. What does the Bodhisatta mean?
It's the name given to the ascetic Gotama before his Enlightenment.
30. What does Nibbana mean?
It means Enlightenment or Awakening. It's the total destruction/cessation of all sufferings or supreme happiness, the highest goal for those who practise the Noble Eightfold Path.
